# NotebookLM prompt: Find sources for the Environmental Field Logger paper

Copy the text below into NotebookLM when you add sources or ask it to find/generate sources for your INF 148 final report.

---

## Prompt (paste this into NotebookLM)

```
I am writing a 5–10 page written report for a university IoT course (Designing for the Internet of Things). My project is an **Environmental Field Logger**: an ESP8266-based device (SparkFun Blynk Board) with multiple sensors that sends data to the Blynk cloud app and to a local Node.js web dashboard.

**What I need sources for:**

1. **Social need and motivation**
   - Why environmental monitoring matters (indoor/outdoor, field deployments, education, citizen science, agriculture, smart buildings).
   - Who benefits from low-cost or DIY environmental sensing (researchers, students, gardeners, educators).
   - Any news articles, reports, or scientific papers that argue for the importance of environmental monitoring or IoT in these contexts.

2. **Competitors and related work**
   - Commercial environmental monitors, weather stations, or IoT kits (e.g. commercial soil/weather stations, other Blynk-based projects, Arduino/ESP environmental projects).
   - How DIY or educational IoT solutions compare to commercial ones (cost, flexibility, data ownership, local vs cloud).
   - Academic or industry papers on IoT for environmental monitoring, smart agriculture, or educational sensing.


Please find and prioritize sources that are:
- Peer-reviewed papers, technical reports, or reputable articles (not just marketing).
- Relevant to environmental monitoring, IoT in education, or small-scale/DIY sensing.
- Useful for citing in the “need,” “competitors,” “security/data,” and “how our system fits the IoT vision” sections of the report.
```
